2019 Playoff Team Performance Comparisons

Team Performances

For a team comparison, comparing the Shock's damage and healing done to another team would have easily shown the teams' player differences, but not all damage or healing done can truly capture a team's performance. Emphasizing team performances, another statistical measurement used in game is the Objective time. The Objective time is counted based on a player's standing on the main point/objective. This measurement better presents a team's performance for comparison as it allows teams more possession of the point after each battle. In other words, the more battles a team wins on the objective, the more possession of the objective and time alotted proves the stronger team. Here displays the San Francisco Shock's performance compared to another team, in this case, against the Vancouver Titans during the 2019 Grand Finals.

Each team's Objective time (in seconds) is the sum of all players' time of that team depending on the map types. See General Game Info for more information on map types. Comparing the San Francisco Shock Objective time to the Vancouver Titans Objective time, the San Francisco Shock have the higher Objective time on all map types except Assault maps. It can be deduced that he San Francisco Shock have a higher win rate in each team battle for the point for more Objective time, or their capacity for stalling the objective. Stalling means trying to have one or more players stall on the point for more time to have the rest of the team respawn and last another team fight which can ultimately change the tide of a match.
